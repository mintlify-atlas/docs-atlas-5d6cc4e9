This guide will walk you through creating your first AI avatar and generating your first video using Duix Avatar. By the end of this tutorial, you'll have a digital clone of yourself that can speak any text you provide.

## Prerequisites

Before starting, ensure you have:

- Completed the installation for either [Windows](/getting-started/windows-installation) or [Linux](/getting-started/linux-installation)
- All Docker services running (verify in Docker Desktop or with `docker ps`)
- Duix Avatar client application installed and launched
- A short video of yourself (for avatar training)
- A voice sample (for voice cloning)

## Step 1: Prepare Your Training Data

<Steps>
  <Step title="Record a training video">
    Record a video of yourself for avatar training. For best results:
    
    - **Duration:** 1-3 minutes is ideal
    - **Lighting:** Use good, even lighting on your face
    - **Background:** Use a clean, simple background
    - **Camera position:** Face the camera directly
    - **Expression:** Speak naturally with various expressions
    - **Quality:** Use 1080p resolution or higher
    - **Audio:** Clear audio is important for voice cloning
    
    <Note>
      The video will be separated into silent video and audio. The video is used for facial training, while the audio is used for voice cloning.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Optional: Prepare a voice sample">
    If you want to use a separate audio file for voice cloning:
    
    - Record clear speech in a quiet environment
    - Duration: 30 seconds to 2 minutes
    - Format: WAV or MP3
    - Content: Speak naturally with varied intonation
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Step 2: Train Your Avatar Model

<Steps>
  <Step title="Launch the client">
    Open the Duix Avatar application.
    
    Ensure all services are connected (you should see indicators showing the services are online).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create a new avatar project">
    1. Click on "Create New Avatar" or similar option
    2. Give your avatar a name
    3. Upload your training video
  </Step>

  <Step title="Process the training data">
    The system will automatically:
    
    1. Separate your video into silent video and audio
    2. Extract facial features from the video frames
    3. Prepare the audio for voice cloning
    
    This process may take a few minutes depending on video length.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Start training">
    Once processing is complete:
    
    1. Review the processed video preview
    2. Click "Start Training" or similar button
    3. Wait for the training to complete
    
    <Note>
      Training time varies based on your GPU and video length. This can take 15-30 minutes.
    </Note>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Step 3: Clone Your Voice

Voice cloning happens alongside model training using the audio extracted from your video.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Audio extraction">
    The system automatically places your audio in:
    
    - **Windows:** `D:\duix_avatar_data\voice\data`
    - **Linux:** `~/duix_avatar_data/voice/data`
    
    This directory is mapped to the `duix-avatar-tts` Docker service.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Voice training">
    The voice cloning service (running on port 18180) will:
    
    1. Analyze your voice characteristics
    2. Train a voice model capturing your speech patterns
    3. Generate a unique speaker ID for your voice
    
    <Note>
      Voice cloning uses the Fish Speech model and captures context, intonation, and speed characteristics.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Test your cloned voice">
    Once training completes:
    
    1. Navigate to the voice testing section
    2. Enter some test text
    3. Generate audio to hear your cloned voice
    4. Adjust settings if needed
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Step 4: Generate Your First Video

Now that your avatar and voice are trained, you can generate videos!

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a new video project">
    1. Select your trained avatar model
    2. Choose your cloned voice
    3. Create a new video generation project
  </Step>

  <Step title="Input your script">
    Enter the text you want your avatar to speak:
    
    - Type directly in the text editor
    - Paste from another source
    - Upload a text file
    
    <Note>
      Duix Avatar supports 8 languages: English, Japanese, Korean, Chinese, French, German, Arabic, and Spanish.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure synthesis settings">
    Adjust settings as needed:
    
    - **Voice parameters:** Top-P, temperature, repetition penalty
    - **Video quality:** Resolution and quality settings
    - **Synchronization:** Lip-sync optimization level
    
    <Note>
      The default settings work well for most cases. Advanced users can fine-tune these parameters.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Generate the video">
    1. Click "Generate Video" or similar button
    2. The synthesis process will:
       - Generate audio from your text using your cloned voice
       - Synthesize lip movements matching the audio
       - Render the final video with synchronized audio
    
    <Note>
      Video generation typically takes 2-5 minutes depending on video length and GPU performance.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Monitor progress">
    You can monitor the generation progress:
    
    - View real-time progress updates
    - Check the processing queue
    - View estimated completion time
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review and export">
    Once generation completes:
    
    1. Preview your generated video
    2. Check lip synchronization
    3. Export the video to your desired location
    4. Make adjustments if needed
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Technical Details

### Docker Services

Duix Avatar uses three Docker services for video generation:

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="ASR Service" icon="ear">
    **Port:** 10095
    
    Automatic Speech Recognition for processing audio input
  </Card>
  <Card title="TTS Service" icon="volume">
    **Port:** 18180
    
    Text-to-Speech synthesis using Fish Speech
  </Card>
  <Card title="Video Service" icon="film">
    **Port:** 8383
    
    Video synthesis and lip synchronization
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### API Endpoints

For developers who want to integrate Duix Avatar programmatically:

**Voice Synthesis:**
```bash
POST http://127.0.0.1:18180/v1/invoke
```

**Video Synthesis:**
```bash
POST http://127.0.0.1:8383/easy/submit
```

**Progress Query:**
```bash
GET http://127.0.0.1:8383/easy/query?code=<task_code>
```

<Note>
  For detailed API documentation, see the source code in:
  - `src/main/service/model.js`
  - `src/main/service/video.js`
  - `src/main/service/voice.js`
</Note>

## Tips for Best Results

### Video Quality

- Use high-resolution training videos (1080p or higher)
- Ensure good lighting without harsh shadows
- Face the camera directly
- Avoid excessive movement during recording

### Voice Quality

- Record in a quiet environment
- Use a good quality microphone
- Speak naturally with varied intonation
- Include different emotions and speech patterns

### Performance Optimization

- Close unnecessary applications to free up GPU memory
- Process shorter videos for faster results
- Use the lite version if you have limited GPU resources

## Troubleshooting

### Video generation fails

- Check that all Docker services are running: `docker ps`
- Verify GPU is available: `nvidia-smi`
- Check service logs in Docker Desktop or via `docker logs <container_name>`
- Ensure sufficient disk space is available

### Poor lip synchronization

- Use higher quality training videos
- Ensure audio is clear and free of background noise
- Try adjusting synchronization settings
- Retrain the model with better source material

### Voice doesn't sound natural

- Provide longer, more varied voice samples
- Ensure clear audio without distortion
- Adjust voice parameters (temperature, top-P)
- Retrain with better quality audio

### Services won't start

- Verify NVIDIA drivers are installed
- Check Docker is running
- Ensure ports 8383, 10095, and 18180 are not in use
- Restart Docker services
